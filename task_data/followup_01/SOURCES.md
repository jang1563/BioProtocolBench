## Included Sources

### Gold

1. Monod J. *The Growth of Bacterial Cultures*. Annual Review of Microbiology (1949). DOI: 10.1146/annurev.mi.03.100149.002011
   - Used as the canonical conceptual reference for nutrient-dependent bacterial growth.

2. Zwietering MH, Jongenburger I, Rombouts FM, van 't Riet K. *Modeling of the bacterial growth curve*. Applied and Environmental Microbiology (1990). DOI: 10.1128/aem.56.6.1875-1881.1990
   - Used as the canonical growth-curve modeling reference for deciding whether a follow-up trajectory is analyzable.

### Silver

1. Growth and Maintenance of *Escherichia coli* Laboratory Strains
   - URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33484484/
   - Used for the representative ~20 minute doubling time in rich LB medium.

2. Chloramphenicol affects *Escherichia coli* growth rates and bacterial membrane composition
   - URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6992672/
   - Used for the roughly 50% growth-rate reduction at 1.8 uM chloramphenicol, supporting the "real slowdown" ground truth.

3. RqcH supports survival in the absence of non-stop ribosome rescue factors
   - URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8006063/
   - Used for the starting OD600 of 0.05 and 15-minute measurement cadence.

4. The *E. coli* molecular phenotype under different growth conditions
   - URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5394689/
   - Used for the cited 0.05-0.75 fraction-of-maximum OD600 fitting window.

5. The *E. coli* molecular phenotype under different growth conditions
   - URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5394689/
   - Reused for the follow-up troubleshooting rationale when an earlier chloramphenicol trajectory is undersampled.

## Rejected Sources

- General educational growth-curve tutorials without protocol specifics
  - Rejected because they do not meet the minimum Silver requirement for task parameters or fitting-window decisions.

- Non-citable internal notes about company-specific applications
  - Rejected because application framing is not a public scientific source for task ground truth.

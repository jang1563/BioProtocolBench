## Included Sources

### Gold

1. Inoue H, Nojima H, Okayama H. *High efficiency transformation of Escherichia coli with plasmids*. Gene (1990). DOI: 10.1016/0378-1119(90)90336-P
   - Used for the canonical high-efficiency transformation range and foundational chemical-transformation framing.

2. Hanahan D. *Studies on transformation of Escherichia coli with plasmids*. Journal of Molecular Biology (1983). DOI: 10.1016/S0022-2836(83)80284-8
   - Used as a second foundational Gold citation for competent-cell plasmid transformation methodology.

### Bronze

1. NEB 5-alpha Competent E. coli product page
   - URL: https://www.neb.com/en-us/products/c2987-neb-5-alpha-competent-e-coli-high-efficiency
   - Used for the recommended ampicillin selection concentration and vendor-validated efficiency range.

2. NEB High Efficiency Transformation Protocol
   - URL: https://www.neb.com/en/protocols/0001/01/01/high-efficiency-transformation-protocol-c2987
   - Used for the 30-minute ice incubation, 30-second heat shock, 60-minute outgrowth, SOC-vs-LB factor, and shaking-vs-static factor.

3. NEB 5 Minute Transformation Protocol
   - URL: https://www.neb.com/protocols/0001/01/01/5-minute-transformation-protocol-c2987
   - Used for the standard 50-100 uL plating volume guidance.

4. FDA BAM Chapter 3: Aerobic Plate Count
   - URL: https://www.fda.gov/food/laboratory-methods-food/bam-chapter-3-aerobic-plate-count
   - Used for the cited 25-250 colony countable range applied to plate-interpretation checks.

## Rejected Sources

- Wikipedia entry on bacterial transformation
  - Rejected because LabCraft's source policy excludes Wikipedia as a primary source when a direct peer-reviewed paper or authoritative vendor protocol is available.

- General lab blog posts describing heat-shock transformation
  - Rejected because they are unsourced community summaries and do not meet the minimum Bronze requirement for threshold-setting claims.
